View NASA EPIC Images

NASA's Earth Polychromatic Imaging Camera (EPIC) aboard the Deep Space Climate Observatory (DSCOVR) satellite captures stunning, full-color images of Earth multiple times a day from one million miles away. Positioned at the Lagrange point 1 (L1), EPIC monitors Earth’s atmosphere, weather patterns, and environmental changes, providing essential data for climate research and weather forecasting. The vivid images of our planet also serve to increase public awareness of environmental issues and the planet's interconnected ecosystems. Additionally, EPIC captures unique phenomena like solar eclipses, offering valuable insights and enhancing our understanding of Earth’s climate system.
